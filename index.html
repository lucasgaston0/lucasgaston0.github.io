Velvet Bar

Role: 3D Artist, Lightning, Post Processing, Final Lookdev

Team Size: 2

Project Duration: 1 Month

Toolset: Unreal Engine, Substance Painter, Maya, 3DSMax

Velvet Bar is a virtual production environment developed in Unreal Engine 5, designed for a film studio equipped with a large LED volume, similar to those used in The Mandalorian and El Eternauta.

The scene was created collaboratively, focusing on achieving the most cinematic atmosphere through a mix of custom-made and scanned assets, realistic baked lighting, texturing. This project involved a full game-ready asset pipeline â€” modeling and optimizing assets in Maya, texturing with Substance 3D Painter and Megascans, and implementing them in Unreal Engine for real-time performance during film shoots.

Lighting this scene was particularly interesting, since it takes place indoors at night. It was a must to go with baked lighting as we needed to place a lot of light sources together. Reflection captures, lightmass settings, and post-processing were carefully adjusted to mimic the feel of a real bar interior, while keeping realtime optimized for on-set performance.

Since the project was intended for real-time virtual production, performance optimization was a top priority. While Lumen and Nanite are the trend in modern Unreal Engine workflows, we decided to disable them to guarantee optimal performance at the cost of more development time. Multiple assets were baked from high-poly to low-poly models, preserving the quality of details while reducing polygon count. Textures from Megascans were used and optimized its texture size depending on the objects screen space.

Mountain VR Bike

Role: Programmer, Optimization

Team Size: 2

Project Duration: 1 Month

Toolset: Unity, Maya, 3DSMax

Developed in Unity for medical centers, this virtual reality application offers an immersive and realistic evaluation experience compatible with Oculus Quest 2 and 3. It features multiple dynamic environments, incline simulation, and NPC cyclists to enhance realism and engagement.

The experience includes three distinct phases â€” Effort, Recovery, and Final Descent â€” each designed to simulate real physical performance conditions. With advanced optimization techniques, the project ensures smooth and high-quality performance on standalone VR devices while maintaining strong visual fidelity.

Optimization was a crucial challenge in this project, as developing a VR application for a standalone headset that runs smoothly without the support of a powerful PC is no easy task. To achieve optimal performance on devices like the Oculus Quest 2 and 3, we implemented advanced optimization strategies such as object pooling to efficiently manage active elements and memory usage.

The entire playable route was divided into segmented zones, each with entry and exit transitions and 2 to 4 intermediate variations to avoid repetition and maintain visual diversity. Furthermore, a dynamic instance selector system was developed to intelligently alternate assets, ensuring a smooth, natural, and immersive experience within the constraints of a standalone VR headset.

Last but not least, careful optimization of foliage was essential. This environment features dense forests and numerous grass planes, so we had to meticulously manage poly count and draw calls to keep performance optimal. If performance drops, it can cause nausea for users, which could possibly lead them to exit the experience.
